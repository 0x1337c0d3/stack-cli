Step 1 Create your Reminders lists (Mac)
1

Open Reminders app on your Mac

2

In the left sidebar, click Add List (bottom left) or press ⌘ N

If you don't see the sidebar, press ⌘ ⌥ S

3

Create each list below in this order, one at a time

Name it, pick the colour shown, press Return to save

4

After creating all lists, select them all with ⌘ click then right-click → Add Group

Name the group "My System" — keeps everything tidy

5

Switch to Kanban view: View → as Columns or press ⌘ 2

📥 Inbox Everything goes here first — never skip this
⚡ Today Max 5 items — if more, you're overcommitting
📅 This Week Planned but not urgent today
🏠 Home All personal / household tasks
💼 Work Work tasks (personal Mac) — mirror on work Mac
💤 Someday Ideas and tasks with no deadline — use tag #someday

💡 On iPhone: long-press any reminder → Move to List to drag between lists. Add Reminders widget to your home screen for one-tap capture.

Step 2 Create your Notes folder structure (Mac)
1

Open Notes on your Mac

2

In the left sidebar, click the New Folder icon (bottom left) or ⌘ ⌥ N

3

Create these four folders in order

🧠
Daily Scratch Pad
One note per week — free-form thinking, meeting notes, ideas
📁
Projects
One sub-note per active project — plans, links, decisions
🏡
Home & Life Admin
Warranties, login notes, household info, medical records
💡
Ideas & Reference
Anything interesting to revisit — articles, thoughts, research
4

Create your first weekly scratch pad note now

Title it "W14 2026" → this becomes your thinking space for the week

5

On iPad: open Notes → tap the Pencil icon → handwrite freely

Tap your handwriting to get the "Convert to Text" option

💡 Quick capture habit: "Hey Siri, remind me to [thing]" on iPhone → goes to Inbox automatically. Every morning, spend 2 min sorting Inbox into the right lists. That's the whole system.
